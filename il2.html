Welcome to a Lesson on Finland

Every country has symbols that represent its unique character and natural heritage. In this short lesson, you will discover the iconic animal and plant that are deeply rooted in Finnish culture and identity. Are you ready to begin?

Question 1: The National Animal

Chosen by a public vote in 1985, Finland's national animal is a magnificent creature that embodies both strength and solitude. It features heavily in the national epic, the Kalevala, where it is treated with great respect and never called by its real name for fear of summoning its power.

Question 2: The National Flower

Delicate, fragrant, and a sign of approaching summer, Finland's national flower emerges in late spring to create a white carpet across shady woodlands. Its Finnish name, 'Kielo', is also a very popular name for girls. It was officially chosen in 1967.
