We fight for Romania's education

    What happened:

    Thousands of teachers and students have taken to the streets, particularly in Bucharest, to oppose recent government measures affecting the education system. These include increased teaching hours, larger class sizes, school mergers, and the elimination of certain scholarships. Protesters are demanding the resignation of Education Minister Daniel David and the reversal of these policies, which they believe undermine the quality of education.

    ← Media

    What this means for Romanian children:

    More school hours and more children per classroom. A 14-year-old child may have 30 to 32 or more hours of school per week, which is 6 to 7 hours per day. On top of that, students face many tests and a heavy workload, yet teachers often earn less than $1,000 per month.

    What this means for international children:

    A less qualified workforce in the future leads to higher costs for society and the economy.

    How you can help as a Romanian:

    Support teachers in their fight, encourage students’ well-being, and participate in protests.

    How you can help internationally:

    Raise awareness online, support teachers publicly, and share information about the protests.

    We have money for them, but not for education.
